<H2>Auntie Shanty Accessibility

The Auntie Shanty session in Oxford takes place in a historic pub. This means that there are some limits on how accessible the sessions are. This page provides a description of the spaces in which we sing:

The Auntie Shanty session takes place in one of two spaces:\
☀️In the summer, we meet under a covered space in the garden (approximately May to September)\
❄️ In the winter we sing in the Shadow Gallery, a room downstairs in the pub (generally November to March)

 Sessions in spring and autumn may be held in either space. The ‘dates’ [link] page lists the dates of the next sessions and their likely locations.


👩‍🦽Physical access
The Shadow Gallery is at the bottom of a flight of stairs, so there is no step-free access to the session when we are there. 

The Garden: it is physically possible for a person in a wheelchair to access the garden space through the (usually locked) gate at the back of the garden. However, in practice it has been found to be very difficult. Pub staff must lock and unlock the gate, and the route is not always clear of furniture. There are also no wheelchair accessible toilets on site.

There are two steps up from the street into the pub, and a further three steps up to the bar. There are X steps between the pub entrance and the garden.

🚽Toilets
The pub has two toilet areas, one with 'cubicles and urinals', the other with 'cubicles'. Both are accessible to all genders. There are no toilets with step-free access.
The nearest wheelchair-accessible toilets are in the Westgate Centre

🪑Seating
In the garden, seating is in large wide hard chairs (seat width: Xcm, seat height Ycm). Tabletops are Xcm from the ground, with Xcm from the bottom of the tabletop to the floor.

In the Shadow Gallery seating is 'bar stool' type seats with no back support or arm rests. Seats are usually available, although some people may stand at busier sessions

👂Sensory: sound
We're singing sea shanties, so it can get loud. The Shadow Gallery is an enclosed room, which can make sound louder and more concentrated. In the garden space, there is more background noise from other pub users, and the bathroom hand-dryers are sometimes audible from outside. 
In both spaces, there is some background noise from music playing on the speakers in the pub. This is usually quietly audible in the periods between our shanties, but can't be heard when we are singing.

💡Sensory: light: 
The Shadow Gallery has bare bulbs lighting mounted on the wall, which some people have found cause migraines. These fittings are on one wall of the space, so it may help to find a position where these are outside your line of sight.
In the garden some orange-coloured light is emitted by the patio heaters when they are turned on.

🏖 Sensory: breakout
Auntie Shanty takes place at a pub. If you want to take a break from the session, there may be tables in other spaces within the pub or the garden. You are very welcome to take a break from the session and then return. There is no designated ‘quiet space’, and the pub usually has music playing throughout in the main indoor space..

*️⃣Sensory: other
The session can get crowded, especially on busy nights in the Shadow Gallery.

There is no smoking inside the pub. In the garden, we sit in a ‘no smoking’ area, adjacent to the rest of the garden. There are occasional smells of cigarettes and vapes which blow towards us. If you are particularly sensitive to this, we recommend getting a seat closer to the ‘pub end’ rather than the ‘garden end’. 

🌡 Temperature
The garden space has heat lamps which we use, but the temperature varies with weather. It's advisable to bring sufficient warm clothes.

The Shadow Gallery may be cold at the very beginning of the evening, but quickly warms up. Seats directly next to the radiators can become very warm.

🍻 Alcohol
The session takes place in a pub. Other people will likely be drinking alcohol. The bar serves a range of non-alcoholic drinks, and there is no expectation that Auntie Shanty participants will drink alcohol. 

💰 Money
The Auntie Shanty Session is free. We are grateful that the Jolly Farmers allow us to use this space, and encourage participants to support them by spending money at the bar if they are able to. The Jolly Farmers bar accepts both card and cash payments.

🤢😷 If you're feeling unwell, please don't come to the session. Please consider others, and avoid spreading the germs.

⏳️Planning: Start Time
If you're coming to the session for the first time, we recommend arriving at around 6:30.

A core group of organisers aim to arrive at 6pm, but we might be delayed. So if you arrive at 6pm, you may be the only person there. In the first half-hour we might rearrange chairs/tables, adjust temperature in the room, and chat about the session. We start singing when it feels like enough people have arrived.

You can arrive at any time through the session. 

If you arrive earlier than 6, the pub will be open.

⌛️Planning: Finishing Time
You are welcome to leave the session at any point, and there is no expectation that people will stay to ‘the end’. It's often quite an informal ending - depends on who is there and how late they want to stay. We usually finish at around 10:30.
