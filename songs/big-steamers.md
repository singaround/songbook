<span style="color:green">Oh, where are you going to, all you Big Steamers,\
With England's own coal, up and down the salt seas?"</span>\
<span style="color:blue">***"We are going to fetch you your bread and your butter,\
Your beef, pork, and mutton, eggs, apples, and cheese."***

<span style="color:green">"And where will you fetch it from, all you Big Steamers,\
And where shall I write you when you are away?"</span>\
<span style="color:blue">***"We fetch it from Melbourne, Quebec, and Vancouver\
Address us at Hobart, Hong Kong, and Bombay."***</span>

<span style="color:green">"But if anything happened to all you Big Steamers,\
And suppose you were wrecked up and down the salt sea?"</span>\
<span style="color:blue">***"Why, you'd have no coffee or bacon for breakfast,\
And you'd have no muffins or toast for your tea."***</span>

<span style="color:green">"Then I'll pray for fine weather for all you Big Steamers,\
For little blue billows and breezes so soft."</span>\
<span style="color:blue">***"Oh, billows and breezes don't bother Big Steamers\
We're iron below and steel-rigging aloft."***</span>

<span style="color:green">"Then I'll build a new lighthouse for all you Big Steamers,\
With plenty wise pilots to pilot you through."</span>\
<span style="color:blue">***"Oh, the Channel's as bright as a ball-room already,\
And pilots are thicker than pilchards at Looe."***</span>

<span style="color:green">"Then what can I do for you, all you Big Steamers,\
Oh, what can I do for your comfort and good?"</span>\
<span style="color:blue">***"Send out your big warships to watch your big waters,\
That no one may stop us from bringing you food."***</span>

<span style="color:blue">***For the bread that you eat and the biscuits you nibble,\
The sweets that you suck and the joints that you carve,\
They are brought to you daily by All Us Big Steamers\
And if any one hinders our coming you'll starve!"***</span>
